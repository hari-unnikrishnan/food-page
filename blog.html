Upma

Roast Semolina: Heat a pan on the induction cooktop at medium temperature and roast 1 cup of semolina until it turns light golden and aromatic. Sauté Ingredients: In the same pan, add a tablespoon of oil, then add mustard seeds, curry leaves, chopped onions, and green chilies, and sauté until the onions become translucent. Add Water: Pour in 2 cups of boiling water into the pan and season with salt to taste. Combine and Cook: Slowly add the roasted semolina while continuously stirring to prevent lumps, then cover and let it cook for about 3-5 minutes on low heat until the water is absorbed. Fluff Upma: Once cooked, remove the lid, fluff the Upma with a fork, and garnish with chopped coriander leaves and a squeeze of lemon juice before serving.

Puttu and Kadala Curry

Mix Rice Flour and Water: Combine 1 cup rice flour with salt and water until it resembles wet sand. Layer in Puttu Maker: Add grated coconut to the bottom, layer with the rice flour mixture, and top with more coconut. Steam Puttu: Place the puttu maker on an induction steamer or use a pot with a steamer attachment, steam for 7-10 minutes on medium heat. Pressure Cook Kadala (Black Chickpeas): Cook soaked black chickpeas in water with salt on induction set to pressure mode or high heat until tender. Sauté Masala: On medium heat, sauté onions, tomatoes, ginger-garlic paste, and spices (turmeric, chili powder, coriander) in oil. Combine and Simmer: Add cooked chickpeas to the masala, and simmer with coconut milk or water until thick.

Idiyappam with Chicken Curry

Sauté Onions and Spices: Set the induction cooktop to 1200W. Heat oil and add curry leaves, onions, and green chilies. Sauté until onions turn golden brown. Add Tomatoes and Masala: Add chopped tomatoes and ginger-garlic paste. Cook until tomatoes soften. Then add turmeric, chili, coriander powder, and salt. Stir for a few minutes. Cook Chicken: Add the chicken pieces and mix well with the masala. Cook for 5-7 minutes until the chicken turns white. Add Water: Pour 1 cup of water and cook on medium heat (1000W) until the chicken is fully cooked. If using coconut milk, add it towards the end and simmer for a few more minutes. Finish: Garnish with curry leaves and turn off the induction.
